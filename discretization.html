Discretization

We divide the fluid inside the cylinder into thin concentric cylindrical shells of radius r and thickness dr.

Each shell carries fluid at a different velocity — faster at the centre, slower near the walls due to viscosity.

The velocity profile across the pipe cross-section is parabolic — maximum at the centre, zero at the wall.

The total flow rate Q is found by summing (integrating) the contributions of all shells from the centre to the wall.

The idealised cylinder of radius R carries viscous fluid under pressure difference ΔP.
